Compass Method — Strategy Tools

Your Smallest
Viable Audience

The people who will find the most meaning in your work already exist. This tool helps you see them clearly — so you can stop making music for everyone and start making it for someone.

01 — Your Music
What is your music about? Describe the feeling, themes, and emotional world of your work.
Don't worry about genre labels. Think about what it feels like from the inside — the mood, the ache, the energy.
02 — Your Instinct
Who do you already sense your music is for? Even a vague feeling counts.
Maybe it's a type of person, a feeling they carry, a situation they're in. Trust your gut here.
03 — Yourself
Describe yourself — your habits, values, what you care about beyond music.
Your audience is often a mirror. The more honest you are here, the more accurate the result.
04 — Your References
Who are your artistic influences, and who do your current fans remind you of?
Artists, films, books, cultural touchstones — anything that lives in the same world as your work.
05 — Optional Photos
Upload up to four images that feel relevant.
Photos of yourself, your aesthetic, your current audience, your visual world — anything that gives texture to who you are and who shows up for you.
